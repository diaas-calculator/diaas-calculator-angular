How much protein should I eat?

This is a very much debated topic.

We'll try to sum up the various recommendations done by official entities or based on research.

They are expressed as grams of proteins per kg of body mass per day but there are also discussions about this. Some argue it should be based on lean body mass. Obviously if you are 20kg overweight, there is no reason you'd need more protein. For people who are overweight, I'd advise to apply one of the formulas for lean body mass or just use an estimate of their "ideal weight".

We provide below a g of chicken meat equivalent just to give a better idea of what each quantity of protein represents, but of course you don't need to eat that much meat to reach the recommendation as there are plenty of other sources of protein.

To wrap it up, in terms of g of protein / body mass / day

  • 0.83 : For the general population with a low or no physical activity (the lower EAR and RDA have been debated as insufficient for ideal health for various reasons. Also the WHO recommendation of 0.83 is more recent)
  • 1.5 : after 50 years old
  • 1.6 : if you're on a diet with a caloric deficit to prevent muscle loss.
  • 1.2 - 2 : for physically active individuals according to various sports societies but no gain on muscle synthesis has been proven above 1.62
